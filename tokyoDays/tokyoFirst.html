Tokyo days 1 through 7

Tokyo day 1 & 2

The next day I set an alarm for 7am, so that i can get into Tokyo as early as possible and be back on track with my plans. So i packed up and left for Osaka St, there i stopped by a 7/11 and aquired a quick breakfast of coffee and onigiri. One of the big stresses i had was aquiring tickets for the shinkansen, as alot of the websites seem quite convoluted and complicated, but eventually i figured it out, and i got onto the train no problem. Luckily i got a window seat so i had 2.5hrs to watch the amazing japanese countryside whizz by at 300km/h, and while on the train i managed to get a glimpse of Mt Fuji!
once i arrived in Tokyo, i headed straight for the hostel, which is only a 5min walk from the metro, then after a quick break to decide what to do for the day headed back into the city. I decided to do Ueno and Asakusa today, since they are both relatively easy ones but also a nice walk in the sun. Because of that i decided to walk from the hostel into the city, as it was only 40mins, which took my through Akhibara.
Once i got to Ueno, i decided to go into the zoo they had, since it was cheap and i thought it would be nice. Ueno zoo has been in the news recently for having its giant pandas confiscated back to China due to worsening relations between the two nations, and you could really feel the loss of their prized pandas everywhere you go. From panda signs and signs, to the gigantic enclosure they had made for them, but even without them i spent a good 2hrs there. After i continued my exploration of Ueno park and ended at the National Museum, which had relics of their past. Where they paintings, sculptures, aswell as original samurai armour and swords which was sick.
Once i managed to look at all their exhibits, i remembered that i have only eaten a few rice balls about 5hrs ago, so decided to get a bit to eat, luckily there was a food market going on, where i decided to get a bunch of different vegetables and doughy things that were fried in a hot miso soup, it was... something, but filling.
By this point i had managed to explore the entire park and headed for my next location, Asakusa, an older part of tokyo, with many shrines, but the main shrine, Asakusa shrine, is the main attraction, with a long rustic shopping street and cherry blossoms, it was extremely pictureque. However this was the first time where i properly felt the overtourism really hit, with just a wall of people, it was relly difficult to walk down the street to get to the main shrine. But once i got through the people, the shrine itself was beautiful, and didnt feel like the centre of Tokyo at all. By this point my feet were beginning to hurt and it was getting dark, so i decided its best to head home and look for a place to eat. On the metro back i checked out the hostelworld Tokyo chat, and i saw there was a small group of people heading out to eat in a couple hours, consisting of an american, dutch and english person. So i joined them i shimbashi where we found a local resteraunt full of drunk salarymen. We knew we found a good place, it was cheap and really good, we had a few snacks to get going, and then finally a giant hotpot of chicken,pork and lots of different vegetables in a deap meaty soup, it was delicous. We decided that a few drinks would be nice to finish off a good day, so we joined up again with a group of people also looking for a drink. So we met i shibuya and found this tiny bar, that only had space for us, and tried a few of their fruit liquors, whcih were dangerously good.
Being concious that trains finish service at midnight, we headed back after a while. Not until i spoke with the American, Jeff, and we chose that the next day we would meet up again and go to Kamakura. Bed at midnight with an alarm for 8
Kamakura is a small town south, and was the political capital in th 13th century, and its most famous for its giant buddha statue, Kotoku-in, aswell as its bamboo forest.
I met up with Jeff at 930 at a nearby station and got onto the packed trainto Kamakura, luckily the crowds dispersed at Tokyo St, so we got a seat for most of the journy, once there we headed straight for the big statue, but not before taking a detour to another temple to have a look around, where they had 9 meter tall wooden statue of Kannon, the Goddess of Mercy. We also got distracted by many snacks that we had to try.
The buddhe statue is indeed big, and you can pay a small fee to go inside the statue to look at its craftmanship, which feels wrong, being inside a holy statue and looking at the metalwork, kind of ruins the mistique... anyways, once we viewed the statue,we decided to walk over the mountain the is in the middle of the city,which in theory would have been nice, except most of the exits off the mountain were shut due to erosion. so we had to backtrack quite far to find an actual exit. After that adventure it was getting towards lunch time, so we decided to go have some ramen, we found a small local place that was selling huge bowls for only 1300yen, huge pieces of pork and a really good broth, 8/10, and most importantly extremely filling.
It was about a 30min walk to the bamboo forest, so we packed up and headed towards it, the walk itself was a bit boring, along a main road, which nly had a small pavement, but we marched on, and we eventually got there, and we both were, extremely underwhelmed. Weve both seen all the images of the great forest, so i was looking forward to at least 5-10mins of exploring this forest, but no, its just a single loop, that would take someone 1min tops, obviously taking more time due to everyone need "that" photo, from one specific angle to make the forest look vast and mysterious. So that was a bit sad, so we headed back down the road, for another 30mins to get to the station, since we had both completely crashed, and wanted to go home. We both ended up snoozing on the train back, and i ended up napping for over 2 hours once i got home. By that point there wasnt much point going out again, so instead im sitting in the hostel listening to the bar downstairs, and having an early night again. Hopefully by tomorrow the jetlag is completely gone.
