Fuji and Kyoto

Fujikawaguchiko and Kyoto

Today i woke up in Tokyo read to move on to Fuji, i had talked to a few people before hand about the best way to get there and they all seems to say that taking the bus is the best way. So i booked a coach for 11am and packed my bags and left. The bus ride itself was simple and got me just 15min walk away from my hostel, but with my suitcase took closer to 20. It was quite cloudy, and slightly drizzly, so unfornatunely Mt Fuji was no where to be seen, the surrounding mountains were covered in clouds aswelll, which was actually quite majestic.
Fujikawaguchiko is on the shores of a large lake, being the largest in 5 lakes that surround the north side of Mt Fuji. So i decided rent a bike and ride round the lake, and see if there would be a break in the clouds to see Fuji (spoiler, there wasnt). I did hire an electric bike, i know, shame on me, but the bikes are so low that i was struggling to peddle anyways, even with the saddle all the way up. I set off around the lake, stoppng a few times to explore the paths that surround the Lake and me cycling slightly faster than inticipated, decided to cycle up to the next lake in the chain, and see that one too, which added an extra 10km or so.
On the trip back, i stopped by a nature museum that was talking about how they found a fish that they thought was extinct, and how the last known location of the fish was 600km away in another lake. So it was interesting to read about their theories and how they are preserving the fish. There was also a bat cave, but unfornunately it was clsoed at the moment. The whole round trip ended in just over 30km in about 2.5 hours. I was however after that, quite tired, so i decided to stay at the hostel for dinner. Since it seemed to have quite a nce restaurant, (plus happy hour when i sat down, so cheap drinks were a bonus.), I ended up having fried prok, miso soup and rice(obviously). It was all delicious. I had a shower and went to bed not long after, with the dirnks being cheap i ended up sipping on them for quite a while, so it was getting late anyways. Looking at the forecast, im happy i went to fuji then, as it was only going further downhill.

The next day i woke up at around 8 am, the plan was to go to Kyoto, and had looked at busses to reserve to Mishima, where the shinkansen makes a stop, and then bullet train to Kyoto, however this time it wasnt possible and the website didnt like my card. So i quickly got up and walked over the the station, which by this point the light drizzle turned into heavy rain, i did have an umbrella, but was happy that i brought my new rain jacket with me. Turns out though that there were quite alot of people with the same idea as me, and the train station where the bus was was packed, and i had to get the last seat on the 3rd bus leaving , so i had to wait around for an hour and a half in the cold (weather app said felt like -2). When the bus finally arrived and we started driving, once we got out of the valley, it was proper snow that had covered the ground, and the bus had to slow down on the motorway, it was all very scenic and all, just nut exactly the weather i had hoped for. So no Fuji views for me and for the rest of the trip :( . The bus ride was about an hour and a half, and i had an hour in the station to grab lunch before the shinkansen i had booked arrived. The train ride itself was uneventful, and with it being so cloudy outside, the view was gray and dull. Once i arrived in Kyoto i went straight to the hostel, which is a converted traditional house, i am staying in a 10 person room, but it was empty, so was quite weird settling in without anyone around. I then headed off for dinner, where i had okomiyaki, which is a kind of pancake with meats, that you fry on a hot plate. It was very delicious. I then spent a couple hours writing the previous blog and then going to bed. The plan is to explore Kyoto the next day and see what this town has to offer.

Kyoto days 2 & 3

Today was my first full day in kyoto, and i wanted to see as much as possible of the city as i could. Kyoto being an old town, famous for its wooden low houses and winding roads. It gave me almost europe vibes compared to the urbanised sprawl of Tokyo, with houses from so many different decades built side by side, giving a much more interesting feeling and skyline to the city. I was staying in the Gion area, which is one of the older and more picturesque areas, so i started my day by wandering those streets, finding alleyways with less people and enjoying the vibes. After i chose to walk a while to the Kyoto imperial palace, as that was quite an iportant cultural location, as it was where the first Shogun announced the defeat of the last rival warlord leading to the end of the civil war (for some of you with a good memory, i visited the grave of that shogun in Nikko), and was also the location of where the last shogun announced the transfer of power back to the emperor. With the palace being constructed many times, and you could feel how in many heavily fortified buildings how its defences transformed along side the evolution of weaponry. One fun room inside the palace was the first reception room, where on the walls there were paintings of tigers, the only issue was that tigers are not from japan, so all these drawings are made by people who have never seen a tiger, so all the proportions and styles were way off.
I spent about a a couple hours there, and then i chose to continue on to the main shrines in kyoto, with the pagode being the tallest in japan, with it being 55m tall, and the other main shrines holding massive buddha statues (your not allowed to take photos inside the shrines, but they are massive). By thise point i was getting hungry, so stopped by a small restaurant chain and had a japanese hamburger steak, because why not. On my way to the restaurant i saw that there is indeed a tv-tower in Kyoto, so i had to go up and enjoy the view. Once i was back down again, i went over to Fushimi Inari Taisha, or more commonly known as the 1000 torii gates. I think this is one of the few popular landmarks that actually deserves the hype, as there are many kilometres of paths that are packed wth toriis, t felt like i could wander them for hours,and with it being the evening it ended up being deserted but also quite eerie with the gravessurounding you.
I had decided that i would meet up with a few people again for dinner tonight, and it turns out that my french connection from tokyo was also in kyoto, he was already meeting up with soeone else at an arcade, so i decided to tag along. The arcade is a chain across alot of japan called round 1, and the issue was, it felt like ti was too easy to win tokens, and i ended up with almost double my starting reserve (obviously you cannot exchange them back to cash, so its still money lost). At the end we wanted to throw the last 100 or so tokens away doing slots, as thats a sure fire way to burn through the last of them, the issue was, was that my friend won, and we ended up with about 2500 tokens, the machine ran out while depositing our winnings, and we had to get a worker to refill the tokens 😂. AS it was already late, and we realised we hadnt had dinner yet, we ended up giving them away hand over fist to anyone who looked our way, which made alot of peoples days, so it was worth it :) After that we went out for skewers at a place nearby, and they were really good. I did end up home relatively late as we ended up talking for quite a long time, and by the end of the day i had walked about 40k steps and had a giant blister, but it was worth it.

The next day i woke up and the blister hadnt gotten mich better, but i was adament to go somewhere, so i chose Arashyama, which is known for its bamboo forest and monkeys. So i put on my shoes and limped over the the bus. Once there i walked up the mountain to see the monkeys, which are the same species as the ones you see in hot springs up north, excpet here they enjoy the open ground and trees. They really are ruthless though, and you have to feed them from inside a cage or else they will bit your fingers off. But overall they are very cute and fun to watch doing their things. Once i was back down i headed along the river that goes through Arashiyama, over to the bamboo forest, and this time, it was an actual forest, unlike last time, with paths leading everywhere, and was enjoyable to explore these, and actually have space to move aorund. Along one of the paths went along led me out of the forest towards a shrine, with a 15th century garden, and another big palace, but all the sliding doors that are used as walls were open, so i decided i could just look inside without actually going in. By this point though, my foot was beginning to hurt from the blister, and decided its about time that i headed home and rest my foot, so thats what i did, not before grabbing some dinner on the way back at a small ramen store, with the menu being "Large or small?" and "2 pieces of pork or 5-" and for 900yen worth of rich umami broth and other ingredientsi wasnt complaining. Back at the hostel, i spent some time writing the blog and then goign to bed relatively early. Tomorrow will be another adventure out.
